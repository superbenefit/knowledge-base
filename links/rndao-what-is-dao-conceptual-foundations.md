RnDAO's conceptual analysis tackles fundamental question of what constitutes a [DAO](tags/daos.md)—examining boundaries and characteristics distinguishing [decentralized autonomous organizations](tags/daos.md) from other [coordination](tags/coordination.md) forms. As "[DAO](tags/daos.md)" term proliferates, conceptual clarity matters for distinguishing genuinely novel [coordination](tags/coordination.md) from traditional organizations using [blockchain](tags/blockchain.md) for limited functions. The analysis explores core characteristics including [decentralization](tags/decentralization.md) of [decision-making](tags/decisions.md), [autonomy](tags/autonomy.md) from individual control, organizational coherence, and onchain infrastructure. Rather than offering single definition, RnDAO examines [DAO](tags/daos.md) as spectrum with varying degrees of [decentralization](tags/decentralization.md), [autonomy](tags/autonomy.md), and [blockchain](tags/blockchain.md) integration—recognizing that boundary between [DAO](tags/daos.md) and non-[DAO](tags/daos.md) involves judgment calls about what matters most for organizational identity.

### Key Highlights

- **Conceptual Clarity Challenge**: RnDAO addresses confusion where "[DAO](tags/daos.md)" describes vastly different organizational forms from fully autonomous [protocols](tags/protocols.md) to traditional companies with [governance](tags/governance.md) tokens, showing need for clearer conceptual [frameworks](tags/frameworks.md) distinguishing [coordination](tags/coordination.md) approaches.

- **Decentralization Dimensions**: The analysis examines multiple forms of [decentralization](tags/decentralization.md)—[decision-making](tags/decisions.md) authority, resource control, technical infrastructure, geographic distribution—showing that organizations can be [decentralized](tags/decentralization.md) along some dimensions while centralized on others.

- **Autonomy from Individuals**: RnDAO explores organizational [autonomy](tags/autonomy.md) as key [DAO](tags/daos.md) characteristic—[governance](tags/governance.md) continuing regardless of specific participants rather than depending on individual leaders—distinguishing this from legal [autonomy](tags/autonomy.md) or automation.

- **Onchain Infrastructure**: The work examines role of [blockchain](tags/blockchain.md) in [DAO](tags/daos.md) definition, asking whether onchain [governance](tags/governance.md) is constitutive characteristic or merely common implementation approach, and what level of [blockchain](tags/blockchain.md) integration defines [DAO](tags/daos.md) identity.

- **Spectrum Rather Than Binary**: Rather than treating [DAO](tags/daos.md) as binary category, RnDAO proposes spectrum with varying degrees of [decentralization](tags/decentralization.md) and [autonomy](tags/autonomy.md), recognizing that organizations occupy different positions rather than simply being or not being [DAOs](tags/daos.md).

- **Normative Versus Descriptive**: The analysis acknowledges tension between descriptive definitions capturing actual [DAO](tags/daos.md) diversity and normative visions of what [DAOs](tags/daos.md) should aspire to, showing how definitional debates often involve value judgments about ideal characteristics.

### Practical Applications

This conceptual work enables clearer DAO discourse:

- Practitioners can use RnDAO's [framework](tags/frameworks.md) to assess where their organizations fall on [decentralization](tags/decentralization.md) and [autonomy](tags/autonomy.md) spectrums, making intentional choices about [DAO](tags/daos.md) characteristics rather than claiming identity without substantive alignment

- Researchers can apply conceptual clarity when studying [DAOs](tags/daos.md), distinguishing [coordination](tags/coordination.md) forms carefully rather than treating vastly different organizations as single category that obscures important variations

- Participants can evaluate whether organizations claiming [DAO](tags/daos.md) status genuinely distribute [decision-making](tags/decisions.md) and resource control or use terminology for legitimacy while maintaining centralized structures

- [Governance](tags/governance.md) designers can consider which [DAO](tags/daos.md) characteristics serve their goals, making intentional choices about [decentralization](tags/decentralization.md) dimensions rather than assuming all aspects should be maximally distributed

- Educators can use the [framework](tags/frameworks.md) when teaching about organizational forms, helping students understand [DAO](tags/daos.md) as spectrum with distinguishable characteristics rather than treating it as monolithic category
