The DAO Research Collective maintains curated repository of academic research, empirical studies, and analytical work on [DAO](tags/daos.md) [governance](tags/governance.md) and [coordination](tags/coordination.md), addressing gap between promotional Web3 discourse and rigorous examination of how [decentralized organizations](tags/daos.md) actually function. The collection spans peer-reviewed papers, working studies, case analyses, and theoretical [frameworks](tags/frameworks.md) from researchers across disciplines including organization science, political economy, law, and information systems. Rather than advocating particular [DAO](tags/daos.md) models, the repository presents evidence-based analysis examining [governance](tags/governance.md) mechanisms, [coordination](tags/coordination.md) challenges, participation patterns, and organizational outcomes. The collaborative curation approach draws on multiple researchers' expertise to identify quality scholarship, making academic [DAO](tags/daos.md) literature more accessible to practitioners, policymakers, and educators who might otherwise struggle to find rigorous analysis amid promotional content dominating Web3 discourse.

### Key Highlights

- **Academic Research Aggregation**: Repository collects scholarly work on [DAOs](tags/daos.md) from dispersed academic venues, providing centralized access to peer-reviewed research, working papers, and rigorous case studies.

- **Evidence-Based Analysis**: Curation prioritizes empirical research and theoretical [frameworks](tags/frameworks.md) over promotional content, helping users access rigorous examination of how [DAOs](tags/daos.md) actually function versus aspirational claims.

- **Multidisciplinary Coverage**: Collection spans organization science, law, economics, political science, and computer science perspectives on [DAOs](tags/daos.md), reflecting how [decentralized governance](tags/governance.md) draws on insights across academic disciplines.

- **Practitioner Accessibility**: Repository makes academic [DAO](tags/daos.md) research accessible to non-academic audiences including [DAO](tags/daos.md) builders, [governance](tags/governance.md) designers, and policymakers who benefit from scholarly analysis but may not regularly engage with academic literature.

- **Collaborative Curation**: Collective draws on multiple researchers' expertise to identify quality scholarship, leveraging distributed knowledge to surface valuable work across disciplines and publication venues.

- **Living Resource**: Repository continues growing as [DAO](tags/daos.md) research field expands, with curators adding new scholarship as it emerges rather than presenting static snapshot.

### Practical Applications

This repository enables research-informed practice:

- [DAO](tags/daos.md) [governance](tags/governance.md) designers can ground [decisions](tags/decisions.md) in empirical research examining what mechanisms actually work in practice, moving beyond anecdotal examples or speculative claims about [governance](tags/governance.md) innovations

- Academic researchers studying [decentralized organizations](tags/daos.md) can use repository as literature review starting point, accessing curated scholarship across disciplines rather than searching multiple databases and venues

- Policymakers developing [DAO](tags/daos.md) regulations can reference academic analysis examining legal, economic, and organizational dimensions of [decentralized governance](tags/governance.md), informing evidence-based policy rather than regulating based on hype or fear

- Educators teaching about [DAOs](tags/daos.md) can draw on curated research for course materials, accessing scholarly work appropriate for classroom use rather than promotional content or purely technical documentation

- Investors evaluating [DAO](tags/daos.md) projects can supplement due diligence with academic research on [governance](tags/governance.md) patterns, understanding scholarly findings about [coordination](tags/coordination.md) challenges and organizational dynamics
